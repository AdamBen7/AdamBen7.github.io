Profile

Currently, I am the Systems Integration Lead for Project Hydrofly, a senior design team tasked with producing a Vertical Takeoff Vertical Landing (VTVL) Lunar Lander System. This system will be used as a testbed for future astronautics research at the University of Alabama.

This past summer, I worked as a Research Intern in the Aoki And Yokozeki Laboratory, at the University of Tokyo.

I have conducted research in the aerospace field since Fall of 2016 with the Aircraft Rapid Prototyping Laboratory for Dr. Charles O'Neill.

On the side, I work IT for the Honors College and the Randall Research Scholars Program, on-campus.

Career/Research Interests:
  • Guidance, Navigation, and Controls
  • Autonomous Systems
  • Aerospace Systems Engineering
  • Modeling and Simulating
