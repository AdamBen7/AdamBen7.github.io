Double-Aileron Balsa Research Plane

During Summer 2017, I worked on a 3D design for of a model airplane wing. This wing was to be mounted under the fuselage of the Aircraft Rapid Prototyping Lab's current research plane.

Over the course of the following Fall semester, I manufactured the design with the assistance of two freshmen (my mentees from the Randall Research Scholars Program).

    Software Used: Autodesk Inventor 2017
    Airfoil: SD7062
    Span: 86in
    Chord Length: 10in
    1.5degrees Dihedral.
    No Twist, Constant Chord Length
    Materials: Balsa Wood, Basswood, Plywood, Cyanoacrylate(Super Glue), 2-Part Epoxy, Fiber Glass, Servo Motors, High Density Foam, Hex Nuts, Screws, Monokote
Shaping the Leading Edge Using 3D Printed Mold and Wet Balsa Sheet
